Community Health Promoter                        Profile and Expectations

 

The mission of Living Goods is to improve health by equipping Community Health Promoters (CHPs) to provide important health information, services, and products to households in their communities.

 

Services Provided by Living Goods CHPs

Living Goods CHPs are health service providers who can offer treatments for sickness in the home and also better living products to households in their zone of operation.  CHPs serve as a key health resource to a zone of 150-300 households, spending 2+ hours per day visiting homes.  While serving their communities, they earn income by selling health and household products. 

 

How Living Goods Equips CHPs

Living Goods equips CHPs with:

  • Free training on key health and business topics
  • A business-in-a-bag with tools and branding materials
  • A subsidized touch phone with mHealth tools to support the CHP’s health work-with a deposit required up-front.
  • A start-up loan for the purchase of initial products, to be repaid over 3 months
  • Stocking of products at Living Goods branches.
  • Focused group refresher training and on-going field support.

 

The Investment to become a CHP

To operate as a Living Goods CHP, applicants must invest time and resources:    

  • Attend three weeks of base training and pass a certification test demonstrating ability to diagnose and treat childhood illnesses.
  • Pay 30,000 UGX deposit for the use of an android phone (refundable).
  • Upon certification, invest UGX 130,000 in initial inventory to build their business, either cash or 3-month credit with a UGX 40,000 deposit required up-front
  • Commit to spending at least 2 hours per day visiting households in their zone.

 

Profile of a Living Goods CHP

Living Goods seeks motivated candidates who care about serving their community, will invest in learning about health and business, and would like to earn some additional income through selling health and household products.

To be considered, candidates must meet certain criteria:

  • Commitment: Commitment to providing health needs and products to the community.
  • Recommendation: Referred by LC or any other local leader
  • Age: Between 30 and 55 years old
  • Education From P7 to S6
  • Language: Fluent in the language spoken locally and able to read and easily communicate in English
  • Residency: At least 2 year of residency in the zone where the CHP will operate
